Leader responsibilities

  1. Have a good understanding of the reading, maybe write up a short outline of the ideas presented (for yourself).
  2. Identify trouble spots - places where you think people could be confused.
  3. If possible, find counterarguments against Lewis.
  4. Find scriptural references for the ideas presented, where possible.
  5. Prepare a discussion/application question (optional, only if applicable).

Discussion outline

  1. Summarize the reading together by writing up an outline of the text together as a group.
  2. Ask questions, both to clarify and challenge the text.
  3. Discuss possible counterarguments.
  4. Examine the scriptural references presented by the discussion leader.
  5. Discuss discussion questions, if there are any.

Pre-discussion preparation

If possible, prepare by reading the assigned chapters before the discussion. Try to note down places where you were confused or had questions.

If you don't have the time, the discussion is still worth joining. Since the first part of our discussion will be all summarizing, you will not miss much.
