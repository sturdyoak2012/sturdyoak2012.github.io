On humans

As a person who studies science, one trait that I'm constantly developing is my ability to observe the world around me. I look at rocks in the outdoors, and I wonder how they got to where they are now. The cracks in the ground after the dirt dries from a recent rainfall—how do they form? Where does all the sand on the beach come from? I see things in the world that I have trouble explaining, and I look for answers.

Of course, my observations aren't limited to the natural world. I ask questions about humans—who humans are, and why they do what they do.
